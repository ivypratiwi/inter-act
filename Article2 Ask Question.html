ASKING QUESTION IN CLASS

Read the following article to understand how to ask question to your tutor or lecturer. Asking the right question is essential for effective communication.

Always-On Inquiry: Why You Should Be Asking More Questions In Your Classroom

Adapted with modifications from: Nayfeld, I


Students are infinitely curious – they want to understand the world around them, how it all works, where it comes from, and how to interact with it. And yet, research shows that students ask few questions during classes/tutorials. Well, why is effectively asking questions pivotal for learning and successful study outcome?

  1. Questions reveal interest: We ask questions when something said catches our attention, surprises us, or when we hear something we want more information about. Asking almost any question is a clear signal that says “Hey! I am paying attention and I want to know more!”Asking a great question? That’s even more revealing.
  2. Questions reveal gaps in, and strength of understanding: As students, we know that not everything we that is said in the classroom will be understood the first time. Therefore, it make learning and understanding a whole lot easier when we asked a question that can clarify a misunderstanding or explain something in a better way. This also gives a chance to fill in the gaps and likely improve understanding for other students too.
  3. Questions improve recall: Research shows that when you ask a question yourself, you are more likely to remember the information you receive. It makes sense – you are likely asking about something that is interesting or relevant to you, and by asking the question you become personally invested in the answer.
  4. Questions keeps us engaged: A question can take a tutorial in which the tutor is doing the talking to a discussion that is more engaging for the students as they are now personally involved in the knowledge sharing process.
  5. Questions build a foundation for new knowledge: Any subject or topic starts out with broad, foundational information that forms the basis for more in depth or specific knowledge. A basic understanding of how plants grow is essential to the discussion of how dessert plants differ from those growing in rain forests.

A question posed at the right time builds that essential knowledge – with that understanding, the more complex, more interesting knowledge has roots, and the learning keeps on building.
